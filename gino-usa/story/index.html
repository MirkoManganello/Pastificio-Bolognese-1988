Meet Gino

The Journey of a Master Pastaio

From the medieval towers of Bologna to your home kitchen in the United States.

My Roots

Castenaso, Egg Yolks & Infinitely Rolled Sfoglia

I grew up in Castenaso, a small agricultural municipality just east of Bologna. In Emilia-Romagna, pasta is not just food; it is our history, our language, and our pride. As a boy, I watched my grandmother roll out the sfoglia—the delicate, egg-yolk-rich dough—using nothing but a long wooden rolling pin called a mattarello.

She taught me that the secret to the perfect tortellino lay in two things: the egg ratio (exactly one egg per 100 grams of soft wheat flour) and the roughness of the sfoglia, which must catch the sauce perfectly. In 1988, after years of working in local kitchens, I opened my small pastificio, determined to keep that hand-rolled heritage alive.

The Pastificio

Crafted in Castenaso, Checked by Hand

In our Bologna pastificio, our tools are modern but our rules remain strictly traditional. We source our flour from local Emilian mills and use fresh, pasture-raised egg yolks that give our pasta its signature deep-gold color.

We extrude our dough through bronze dies to create a rough, porous texture, and for our filled lines, we wrap the fillings instantly to lock in freshness. Every batch is evaluated by hand. We monitor drying temperature and humidity over hours, ensuring the tortellini remain shelf-stable without ever using artificial preservatives or additives.

The Crossing

Bringing the True Taste of Bologna to America

For years, tourists from the US visited my pastificio, telling me they could never find authentic shelf-stable filled tortellini back home. They were tired of processed, heavy box pasta that tasted nothing like the real food of Bologna.

That is why we launched **Gino USA**. We wanted to bridge the gap between our artisanal pastificio and your dinner table. We ship directly from our family pastificio to our Florida warehouse, ensuring that whether you make a fast Weeknight Cacio e Pepe or a slow Sunday Ragù, you get the exact same pasta that my family enjoys in Castenaso.
