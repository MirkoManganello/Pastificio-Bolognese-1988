Gino's Story

The Hands That Roll the Sfoglia

"I was born in Castenaso, right outside Bologna. In 1988, I decided that the machine-made pasta in supermarkets was losing the soul of the egg dough—the sfoglia. I opened a small shop, infusing the dough with eggs from local farms and filling my tortellini with real mortadella and 24-month Parmigiano. Today, our family pastificio works with the same uncompromising values."

We crossed the Atlantic because real, authentic Bolognese pasta shouldn't be a luxury. We ship it straight to you, preserving the true taste of my home town.

Questions, Answered

How can filled tortellini be shelf-stable without a fridge?
Our slow-drying method — the same one used for premium dried pasta, but adapted to filled sfoglia — removes the moisture that spoilage needs while preserving the structure of the egg dough and the filling. That's why Gino keeps for 16 months in your pantry with no preservatives and no refrigeration.
How long does delivery take, and what does shipping cost?
Orders ship from our Florida warehouse within 24 hours and arrive anywhere in the continental US in 2–4 business days. Shipping is free over $50 — below that, it's a flat $6.95.
How do I cook it? I'm not a chef.
You don't need to be. Ready Rounds just need hot water and 90 seconds. Tortellini and ravioli go straight into boiling water for 3–4 minutes. Every box includes one of Gino's recipe cards if you want to go further.
